# Referenced-source research

Load this only when a video mentions or shows a source that matters to the user’s question, or when a material claim needs external verification.

## Check nearby evidence first

Before broad search, inspect:

1. The exact transcript timestamp.
2. A clear frame of any citation, title, author, logo, chart, or URL.
3. The video description and direct links.
4. Creator corrections or source comments when relevant.

Comments can discover a source but are not authority for its findings.

## Delegate a bounded packet

Prefer a subagent for uncertain web exploration so failures and irrelevant pages do not flood the main context.

Provide:

~~~text
one precise research question
video claim and timestamp
transcript excerpt
relevant frame path
candidate title, author, organization, date, or terms
links already found
primary-source preference
bounded search budget
~~~

Ask for:

- exact, probable, or unresolved identification.
- The canonical source.
- Brief match reasoning.
- Findings relevant to the video claim.
- Contradictions or corrections.
- Remaining uncertainty.

For technical, scientific, legal, medical, or financial claims, prefer original research and current primary documentation. Clearly separate source identification from whether the source actually supports the speaker’s interpretation.

Keep raw browsing history and unhelpful dead ends out of the main synthesis unless they explain an unresolved result.
